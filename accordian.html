Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) −

DBT is a popular method that can help you regulate intense emotions, improve relationships, and build skills for mindfulness, distress tolerance, and emotional balance. If you're struggling with overwhelming emotions or destructive and impulsive behaviors, this therapy can be especially beneficial.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) +

Another common method, CBT focuses on identifying and changing the unhelpful thought patterns that contribute to emotional distress. By shifting these patterns, you can start to build the coping skills needed to improve daily life.

Exposure and Response Prevention Therapy (ERP) +

Especially beneficial for those struggling with anxiety and OCD, this therapy gradually helps you face feared situations. You'll learn to resist the urge to engage with unhealthy coping mechanisms.

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) +

If you're the type who likes to avoid your negative thoughts and emotions, ACT can help you face them without avoidance or conflict. By clarifying your personal values, you can make decisions that align with what matters most in your life.

Emotion-Focused Therapy (EFT) +

Deep emotional experiences can be overwhelming, but EFT helps you better understand and work through these feelings. The process fosters emotional connection, healing, and stronger interpersonal relationships.

Motivational Interviewing +

Struggling with self-doubt? This client-centered approach helps you build the motivation you need to make positive life changes. Overcome ambivalence and start building the confidence you need to grow.

Interpersonal Therapy (IPT) +

IPT focuses on improving communication and relationships while addressing the negative patterns that contribute to emotional distress. It’s effective for individuals experiencing depression, grief, or life transitions.
